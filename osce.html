ABCDE Approach

  • A – Airway: Ensure airway is patent
  • B – Breathing: RR, O2 saturation
  • C – Circulation: Pulse, BP
  • D – Disability: AVPU / GCS
  • E – Exposure: Full exam

SBAR

  • S – Situation
  • B – Background
  • A – Assessment
  • R – Recommendation

System Examination

  • General inspection
  • Vitals
  • Cardiovascular exam
  • Respiratory exam
  • Abdominal exam
  • Neurological exam
🧠 OSCE Scenarios (Click to open)
Shortness of breath
  • Breathless
  • RR ↑
  • O2 sat ↓

Answer: ABCDE, oxygen, consider asthma/pneumonia

Chest pain
  • Central chest pain
  • Radiates to arm
  • Sweating

Answer: MI → ECG, aspirin, oxygen

Fever + cough

Task: Assess and give diagnosis

  • Fever
  • Productive cough
  • Chest pain

Answer:

  • Pneumonia likely
  • Check vitals
  • Start antibiotics
Unconscious patient

Task: Initial assessment

  • Unresponsive
  • Low BP

Answer:

  • ABCDE immediately
  • Check glucose
  • Secure airway
Severe abdominal pain

Task: Assess cause

  • Sudden severe pain
  • Guarding

Answer:

  • Consider peritonitis
  • Urgent surgical review
Head injury

Task: Assess neurological status

  • History of trauma
  • Confusion

Answer:

  • Check GCS
  • Look for red flags
  • Urgent CT if needed
Child with fever

Task: Assess severity

  • Fever
  • Lethargy

Answer:

  • Check ABCDE
  • Rule out sepsis
Diabetic patient confused

Task: Identify cause

  • Known diabetes
  • Confusion

Answer:

  • Check blood glucose
  • Consider hypoglycemia
Patient with leg swelling

Task: Assess cause

  • Unilateral swelling
  • Pain

Answer:

  • Consider DVT
  • Send Doppler ultrasound
Vomiting + dehydration

Task: Management

  • Vomiting
  • Dry mouth

Answer:

  • Assess dehydration
  • Give fluids (oral/IV)
