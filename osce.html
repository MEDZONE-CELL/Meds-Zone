🩺 OSCE Guide

SBAR (Patient Presentation)
  • S: Situation – Who is the patient? What is happening?
  • B: Background – Relevant history
  • A: Assessment – Your findings
  • R: Recommendation – What you plan to do
History Taking (General)
  • Presenting complaint
  • History of presenting complaint
  • Past medical history
  • Drug history
  • Family history
  • Social history
  • Review of systems

  • Blood pressure
  • JVP
  • Precordial inspection
  • Palpation (apex beat)
  • Auscultation
Respiratory Examination
  • Inspection

  • Auscultation

Neurological Examination

🚨 Emergency Approach (ABCDE)

A – Airway

  • Is the patient talking? → airway is patent
  • Look for obstruction (vomit, blood)
  • Open airway (head tilt–chin lift / jaw thrust)
  • Suction if needed

B – Breathing

  • Check respiratory rate
  • Check oxygen saturation
  • Look for distress
  • Give oxygen
  • Auscultate chest

C – Circulation

  • Check pulse
  • Check blood pressure
  • Look for bleeding
  • Insert IV line
  • Give IV fluids

D – Disability

  • Check consciousness (AVPU / GCS)
  • Check blood glucose
  • Check pupils

E – Exposure

  • Fully examine patient
  • Look for injuries
  • Keep patient warm
