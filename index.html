🧠 Clinical Cases

Case 1: Pulmonary TB with Shock

Patient: 33-year-old male

Presentation: Generalized body pain and non-productive cough for 2 weeks

Vitals:

  • BP: 83/42 mmHg
  • Pulse: 110 bpm

Impression:

Pulmonary TB with possible septic shock

Reasoning:

  • Low BP → hypotension
  • Tachycardia → compensatory response
  • Infection → possible sepsis

Management:

  • IV fluids
  • Broad-spectrum antibiotics
  • GeneXpert testing
Case 2: Severe Anemia in HIV

Patient: 28-year-old female

Presentation: Fatigue, dizziness, shortness of breath

Vitals:

  • BP: 100/60 mmHg
  • Pulse: 105 bpm

Findings:

  • Pale conjunctiva
  • Hb: 6.5 g/dL
  • HIV positive on ART

Impression:

Severe anemia

Reasoning:

  • Low Hb explains fatigue
  • HIV → chronic disease cause

Management:

  • Blood transfusion
  • Iron studies
  • Continue ART
