Case 1: Suspected Pulmonary TB with Shock

Patient: 33-year-old male

Presentation: Generalized body pain and non-productive cough for 2 weeks

Vitals:

  • BP: 83/42 mmHg
  • Pulse: 110 bpm

Impression:

Pulmonary TB with possible septic shock

Reasoning:

  • Low BP → hypotension
  • Tachycardia → compensatory response
  • Infection (TB) → possible sepsis

Next Steps:

  • Calculate MAP
  • Start IV fluids
  • Send labs (FBC, cultures)
  • Confirm TB (GeneXpert)
